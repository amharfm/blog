> What if I fall ill?

My recommendation (learning from my personal experience), is to keep in touch with your classmates. TYKS and FSHS are probably far from your dorm. Update: The FSHS' emergency call now handles calls in English language

> To get the Student Card

1. Complete your TYY/Student payment

2. Secure the receipt. Because it is a temporary replacement for the card, discounts can be accessed by showing the receipt to the cashier

3. If you already have an active school email address, access digital card that is downloadable via Frank.fi.

4. If you want a physical one, it can also be ordered through the same website.

Update: Yes, our TYY payment and campus' system to recognize the payment takes time. But, I notice some of you might be waiting for -relatively- too long; thus to contact the admission office is a smart move. To wait patiently is too a smart move.

Do not forget to update the Frank.fi app. This is not a free service anymore

> 1e Passport Photo

There's a passport photo booth which serves digital version of the photo, in Raisio. I went there as I needed to go to Raisio's Police Station to extend my Residence Permit

> Finnish ID / personal identity code

The code is actually additional codes next to your birthdate (as far as I know) that is obtained through the procedure in local register office as a foreigner. To get that, bring the residence permit along with passport and info about complete address of where you live in Turku (or other place). Perhaps it is wise to have a finnish phone number too at this point. They will register you and letters, bills, etc will come to the address you use in the form.

By the way, the office has been moved. This is the google maps link: Maistraati, and we do not need to make an appointment for this (in Turku).

> Where can I buy a sim card?

SIM cards are usually available in minimarkets (K-Market, R-kioski, etc.). Instruction to register a new number is in Finnish but not very unfamiliar to how a number is registered usually. Later, recharging the credits could be done in the same place, with Euro unit (for example, only 10e).

> Where should I go to get a receipt for student union fee?

From what I understand, you actually can email the receipt (when you did the payment) to either Enni or Johanna (via admission@utu.fi), and ask for the receipt. Or, if the system has recognized your payment, you could try to access the digital version first through the app. It is on Frank.fi website. What I meant with digital version was a student card. The physical one can be requested in frank.fi too

Admission office is located in the office named DISCO or the international students affair, near Feeniks/main library. Another solution is to email TYY themselves.

> Information Recap for all (I)

1. Registration ends at the end of August, but July 10th is the last day for new students applying to the program. However, usually courses are open around August. I would be very glad to share my experience in studying courses I took last semesters. Hopefully it helps your Study Plan later.

2. To do registration, which means to state that you are attending the following semester, you also need to pay for student union fee (around 107 e for 2 semesters).

Let me know if anyone gets difficulties in processing this payment. I myself did not have a Finnish bank account back then, so I paid through my own banking transfer. Two semesters have passed and now I realized that having the bank account (and its online banking feature) is very useful.

3. Also, we all will meet at the Orientation Day (August 29-31), in which most information about new students will be given. Last year, my tutor had us meet up to go to buy bus ticket package and other things. I wonder if we should also meet together some day in August

4. "Maistraati"/Local registration office service will give you Finnish ID/acknowledge your residence permit. It is especially if you already have a place where you will stay in Turku, and an accessible phone number.

5. Please re-check any documents needed to be delivered once you're here, from academic to non-academic-related ones.

6. If you are unfamiliar, I feel that access to information in Finland is mostly text-based. Public service is usually handled in appointment-based.
